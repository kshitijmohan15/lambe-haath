🔹 MASTER RE-USABLE PROMPT

(For Article-wise Time Chart & Flowchart Preparation under CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965)

Context & Legal Premise (Mandatory):
The Articles of Charge contained in ANNEXURE–I alone constitute the charges in law under Rule 14 of the CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965.
The Statement of Imputation under ANNEXURE–II does not create any independent charge and is to be used only for explaining, elaborating, or supplying particulars to the Articles of Charge already framed in ANNEXURE–I.

You must strictly adhere to this legal position while performing the tasks below.

TASK–1: Identification of Charges

Identify each distinct Article of Charge as framed in ANNEXURE–I.

Treat each Article independently.

Do not mix facts, events, or allegations across different Articles of Charge.

TASK–2: Extraction of Relevant Facts from ANNEXURE–II

For each individual Article of Charge under ANNEXURE–I, do the following:

From ANNEXURE–II, extract only those facts, dates, actions, omissions, file movements, approvals, communications, and events which:

Directly relate to that specific Article of Charge; and

Are relied upon to explain or support that Article.

Ignore and exclude:

Facts not traceable to that Article of Charge;

General background narration not linked to the charge;

Any inference or assumption not expressly stated in ANNEXURE–II.

TASK–3: Preparation of Article-wise Time Chart

For each Article of Charge separately, prepare a chronological time chart in tabular form with the following mandatory columns:

| Sl. No. | Date / Period | Event or Action | Officer / Section Involved | Document / File Reference (if any) | Source (Para of Annexure-II) |

Instructions:

Arrange events strictly in chronological order.

Use neutral, factual language—do not add conclusions or opinions.

If a date is approximate or inferred in ANNEXURE-II, clearly state so.

Each entry must be traceable to a specific paragraph of ANNEXURE-II.

TASK–4: Logical Sequencing for Charge Analysis

After the time chart for an 

Article

 is prepared:

Identify the alleged decision-making points, acts of omission/commission, and linkages relied upon by the Disciplinary Authority.

Highlight:

Where the Charged Officer is alleged to have acted;

Where inaction or failure is alleged;

Any gaps, delays, or assumptions apparent from the timeline.

TASK–5: Preparation of Pictorial Flowchart (Article-wise)

For each Article of Charge, convert the corresponding time chart into a separate pictorial flowchart with the following characteristics:

The flowchart must:

Start from the earliest relevant event;

Progress step-by-step to the alleged misconduct;

End at the specific allegation attributed to the Charged Officer.

Use clear nodes such as:

Receipt of file / proposal

Action taken / not taken

Role attributed to Charged Officer

Alleged violation / misconduct

Ensure:

One flowchart per Article of Charge;

No overlapping of events between different Articles;

The flowchart visually reflects causation as alleged, not presumed guilt.

TASK–6: Output Format

For each Article of Charge, present the output in the following order:

Heading:
Article of Charge – I / II / III (as applicable)

Chronological Time Chart (Table)

Brief Narrative Explanation (2–3 paragraphs) explaining how ANNEXURE-II particulars are being used only to elaborate ANNEXURE-I

Pictorial Flowchart (diagram / mermaid / visual schematic as requested)

Critical Constraints (Must Be Followed):

Do not re-frame or improve the charge.

Do not supply missing links on behalf of the department.

Do not import external facts or case law unless expressly asked.

Maintain disciplinary-proceeding neutrality, suitable for defence analysis and cross-examination planning.
