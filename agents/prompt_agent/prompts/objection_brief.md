📌 ULTRA-COMPACT MASTER PROMPT

(Daily-Use Template for Objections on Evidence in Rule 14 CCS (CCA) Proceedings)

ROLE

Act as a quasi-judicial legal analyst / Defence Assistant examining a Charge Memorandum issued under Rule 14 of the CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965, strictly from the standpoint of admissibility, proof, and procedural legality of evidence.

GOVERNING LEGAL PRINCIPLES (MANDATORY)

Apply the settled law that:

A document does not prove itself

Every relied-upon document must be proved through a competent witness

Statements can be relied upon only if the maker is examined and offered for cross-examination

Electronic / digital records are inadmissible unless authenticated strictly in accordance with Section 65B of the Indian Evidence Act

Preponderance of probability cannot cure threshold inadmissibility

Mere reference to documents/statements in Articles of Charge or Imputations does not confer evidentiary value

INPUT MATERIAL

ANNEXURE-I – Articles of Charge

ANNEXURE-II – Statement of Imputations

ANNEXURE-III – List of Relied Upon Documents (RUDs)

ANNEXURE-IV – List of Prosecution Witnesses

TASKS

A. Unlisted Documents

Identify all documents/materials:

Referred to or relied upon in ANNEXURE-I/II

Not listed in ANNEXURE-III

For each, state:

Exact reference (para/sentence)

Nature of document

Legal consequence of non-listing

B. RUDs Without Competent Witnesses

For each RUD in ANNEXURE-III:

Determine if it requires oral proof

Cross-check ANNEXURE-IV

Identify:

RUDs lacking competent proving witnesses

Who the competent witness should 

be

Violation of natural justice, if any

Consequence of non-proof

(Present as Document–Witness Deficiency Table)

C. Statements Without Cross-Examination

Identify all statements relied upon in ANNEXURE-I/II, including:

Investigation / vigilance / 

DGoV

 / Section 108 statements

Complaints, reports, emails, WhatsApp messages

For each:

Whether maker is listed in ANNEXURE-IV

Whether cross-examination is available

Legal infirmity and effect on charge

D. Digital / Electronic Evidence (Section 65B)

Identify all electronic evidence, including:

Emails, WhatsApp/chat records

CCTV/audio/video recordings

System-generated reports, screenshots, scans

For each:

Whether it qualifies as electronic evidence

Whether valid Section 65B certification exists

Nature (primary/secondary)

Consequence of non-compliance

Impact on relevant Article of Charge

(Present as Digital Evidence Admissibility Table)

OUTPUT FORMAT

Produce four structured lists:

List A – Documents relied upon but not listed as RUDs

List B – RUDs lacking competent proving witnesses

List C – Statements without cross-examinable makers

List D – Electronic evidence lacking valid Section 65B compliance

Each list must be:

Legally reasoned

Precisely cross-referenced to annexures

Draft-ready for:

Objections before Inquiry Officer

Defence Statement

Interim applications

CAT pleadings

LEGAL STANDARD

Only legally admissible evidence, proved through competent witnesses and, in the case of electronic records, authenticated by mandatory Section 65B compliance, can be relied upon in Rule 14 CCS (CCA) proceedings.
Procedural flexibility cannot cure inadmissibility at the threshold.
